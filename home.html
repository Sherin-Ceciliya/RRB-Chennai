About Us

The Railway Recruitment Board (RRB) is a statutory body functioning under the Ministry of Railways, Government of India. It is entrusted with the responsibility of recruiting eligible candidates for various posts in the Indian Railways through a fair, transparent, and merit-based selection process.

RRBs were initially known as Railway Service Commissions when they were formed in 1942. In 1985, they were renamed as Railway Recruitment Boards. There are currently 21 RRBs across India, each catering to specific geographical zones. RRB Chennai is responsible for recruitment in the southern region, particularly Tamil Nadu and surrounding areas.

The RRBs conduct recruitment for a wide range of positions across different categories such as:

  • Group C posts including Technical and Non-Technical staff
  • Junior Engineers (JE), Depot Material Superintendent (DMS), and Chemical & Metallurgical Assistant (CMA)
  • Assistant Loco Pilots (ALP) and Technicians
  • Traffic Assistants, Station Masters, and Goods Guards
  • Paramedical and Ministerial categories

The recruitment process includes Computer-Based Tests (CBT), Skill Tests, Document Verification, and Medical Examinations, depending on the nature of the post. RRBs follow strict protocols to maintain the sanctity of the process, using advanced technology to ensure accuracy and prevent malpractices.

The Railway Recruitment Control Board (RRCB), headquartered in New Delhi, supervises the activities of all RRBs and issues guidelines to bring uniformity across the country. It also monitors the resolution of complaints, legal cases, and administrative coordination.

RRB Chennai is committed to upholding the principles of equal opportunity, integrity, and efficiency in public service by recruiting the best-suited candidates to contribute to one of the world’s largest and busiest railway networks.
