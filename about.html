About Us

Railway Recruitment Board (RRB), Madras (now Chennai) functions under the Ministry of Railways, Government of India. It is primarily responsible for recruiting the working and supervisory staff (Group 'C') required for Chennai, Salem, and Tiruchirappalli Divisions, as well as the Head Quarters office of Southern Railway (SR) and Integral Coach Factory (ICF), Chennai.

Selection Procedure

RRB calls for eligible applicants through "Employment News" (A publication of the Government of India). The employment notification is also published on the Internet website. Applications are scrutinized for eligibility, and eligible candidates are called for a written examination, with call letters sent a month in advance.

In most categories, there is no interview after the written examination. Some categories may have a second stage written examination. For operational safety roles, candidates undergo a psychological test. After the written exam, applications of successful candidates are verified, followed by counselling and scrutiny of original documents. A panel of candidates is recommended to Southern Railway / Integral Coach Factory for vacancies. Candidates must pass a medical examination before appointment.

The results are made available:

  • On the Notice Board of RRB/Chennai
  • Through the Internet website
  • In "The Employment News"
  • To successful candidates via post

The selection process is based on merit and subject to the rules issued by the Ministry of Railways, including reservations for SC, ST, OBC, and ex-servicemen. The recruitment system functions impersonally, with no scope for corruption. Malpractices are strictly dealt with.

Overall policy guidelines for all Railway Recruitment Boards in India are laid down by the Railway Recruitment Control Board (RRCB), New Delhi.
